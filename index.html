How do you work with clients?

I believe great design comes from close collaboration with stakeholders, continuous iteration, and validation with real users.
Projects begin with a clearly defined scope and set of requirements. I work with stakeholders to break these requirements into user journeys, UX flows, and detailed requirement mappings. I provide visual references (moodboard), create wireframes and prototypes to validate structure, interactions, and edge cases.
High-fidelity designs are produced in Figmaâ€”deliverables include production-ready screensand reusable components. I also collaborate closely with developers ensuring alignment from design to implementation.

What tools do you use?

Figma is the core of my design workflow, from early exploration to high-fidelity production.
I rely on it not just for UI design, but also for building and maintaining design systems, documenting decisions, and collaborating closely with engineers. FigJam (and occasionally Whimsical) to map ideas, run design workshops, and align stakeholders around problems before moving into solutions.
I use Rotato to create clean, realistic device mockups, Loom to present designs asynchronously with the team, and also DaVinci Resolve for product demo reels, walkthroughs, and short product narratives. Outside of interface design, I use Procreate for sketching, drawing, and illustration.

How do you collaborate with developers?

Figma Dev Mode + tidy design documentation. I ensure layouts, spacing, typography, and component behavior are easy to inspect. I design with real constraints in mind and prioritize reusable patterns over one-off solutions.
When needed, I clarify edge cases, states, and responsive behavior to reduce ambiguity during build time. Clear communication and shared understanding are just as important as visual quality.
